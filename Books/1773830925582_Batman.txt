Batman (real name Bruce Wayne) is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by DC Comics.
Common nicknames include The Dark Knight, The Caped Crusader, and The World's Greatest Detective.

Quick facts:

Created by: Bob Kane and Bill Finger.

First appearance: Detective Comics #27 (1939).

Real name: Bruce Wayne.

Occupation: Billionaire businessman, philanthropist, vigilante.

Base of operations: Gotham City.

Known for: Intelligence, detective skills, martial arts mastery, advanced technology, and strategic thinking.

Origin story:

Bruce Wayne was born into the wealthy Wayne family in Gotham City.

As a child, he witnessed the murder of his parents, Thomas Wayne and Martha Wayne.

This traumatic event inspired him to dedicate his life to fighting crime.

He traveled the world to train in martial arts, science, criminology, and detective work.

Returning to Gotham, he adopted the symbol of a bat to strike fear into criminals and became Batman.

Allies:

Robin – Batman’s trusted sidekick and crime-fighting partner.

Alfred Pennyworth – Bruce Wayne’s loyal butler and advisor.

Commissioner James Gordon – Key ally within the Gotham City Police Department.

Batgirl – Member of the Bat-family and skilled crime fighter.

Major enemies:

Joker – Batman’s most famous and dangerous enemy.

Catwoman – Skilled thief and sometimes ally.

Penguin – Crime boss of Gotham.

Riddler – Criminal mastermind known for puzzles and riddles.

Two-Face – Former district attorney turned villain.

Equipment and abilities:

Batman has no superpowers, but relies on training and intelligence.

Uses advanced gadgets like the Batarang, grappling gun, and smoke bombs.

Operates from the Batcave, a secret base beneath Wayne Manor.

Drives the famous Batmobile and uses other specialized vehicles.

Master of multiple martial arts and detective investigation.

Style and legacy:
Batman is widely regarded as one of the most iconic superheroes in comic book history. Unlike many heroes, he relies on intellect, discipline, and technology rather than supernatural powers. His dark and complex personality, dedication to justice, and relentless fight against crime have made him a symbol of resilience and determination in popular culture.
